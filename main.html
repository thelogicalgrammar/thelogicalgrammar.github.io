I am a PhD student in the Centre for Language Evolution at the University of Edinburgh. I am supervised by Simon Kirby and Marieke Schouwstra.

In my PhD, I study the evolutionary mechanisms that lead to the emergence of cross-linguistically stable properties in the semantics of scalar language, including gradable adjectives, quantifiers, and modals. In particular, I look at two universals of scalar semantics, monotonicity and extremeness. If you are also interested in these topics, send me an email!

Research interests: gradable adjectives; quantifiers; conceptual spaces; language evolution; iterated learning; Bayesian cognitive modelling; Bayesian statistics

Other academic interests: free energy principle; predictive processing; natural language ontology
