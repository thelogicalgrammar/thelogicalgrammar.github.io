I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute for Language, Cognition, and Computation in Amsterdam with Jakub Szymanik, as part of the Cognitive Semantics and Quantities project. In my postdoc at the ILLC, I look at the semantics and pragmatics of quantifiers from a Bayesian and evolutionary perspective.

I finished my PhD in the Centre for Language Evolution at the University of Edinburgh in 2020. I was supervised by Simon Kirby and Marieke Schouwstra. In my PhD, I studied the evolutionary mechanisms that lead to the emergence of cross-linguistically stable properties in the semantics of scalar language, including gradable adjectives, quantifiers, and modals. In particular, I look at two universals of scalar semantics, monotonicity and extremeness.

Research interests: gradable adjectives; quantifiers; conceptual spaces; language evolution; iterated learning; Bayesian cognitive modelling; Bayesian statistics

Other academic interests: free energy principle; predictive processing; natural language ontology
